Municipal Water Supply SOP
Document ID: SOP-2024-WS-001
Effective Date: January 1, 2024

1. WATER QUALITY STANDARDS

All water supplied to citizens must meet WHO drinking water quality standards.

1.1 Testing Requirements
- Daily testing: pH, turbidity, chlorine levels
- Weekly testing: Bacteriological parameters
- Monthly testing: Heavy metals, pesticides
- Quarterly testing: Comprehensive chemical analysis

1.2 Acceptable Ranges
- pH: 6.5 - 8.5
- Turbidity: < 5 NTU
- Residual Chlorine: 0.2 - 0.5 mg/L
- Total Coliform: 0 CFU/100mL

2. COMPLAINT HANDLING

2.1 Response Timeline
- Acknowledgment: Within 2 hours of complaint
- Inspection: Within 24 hours
- Resolution: Within 3-5 business days
- Critical issues: Within 12 hours

2.2 Complaint Categories
- No water supply
- Low pressure
- Water quality issues
- Pipe leakage
- Meter problems

3. MAINTENANCE SCHEDULE

3.1 Regular Maintenance
- Tank cleaning: Every 6 months
- Pipeline inspection: Quarterly
- Pump maintenance: Monthly
- Valve testing: Bi-annually

3.2 Emergency Procedures
In case of pipeline burst:
1. Isolate affected section immediately
2. Notify residents via SMS/loudspeaker
3. Deploy tankers for temporary supply
4. Repair within 24 hours
5. Test water quality before restoration

4. COMPLIANCE

All personnel must:
- Follow standard operating procedures
- Document all actions
- Report issues immediately
- Maintain cleanliness standards
- Complete mandatory training annually

Violations may result in disciplinary action.

END OF DOCUMENT
